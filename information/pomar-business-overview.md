# Pomar — Complete Business Overview

**Tagline:** *Do pomar à mesa* — from the orchard to the table.
**One-liner:** A seasonal farm-to-table provisions brand from Portugal's southwest coast — a market, a small kitchen, and a weekly harvest box, all sourced from growers down the road.

> **On the name:** *Pomar* is the Portuguese word for an orchard — a planted, tended place that gives back season after season. It carries the whole brand in one word: rootedness, patience, and the simple generosity of land that's cared for.

---

## The story

Pomar started in 2021 when Inês Tavares, a chef who'd spent a decade cooking in Lisbon and San Sebastián, moved back to the Algarve and found she couldn't easily buy the produce she'd grown up with — the figs, apricots, and peppers were all leaving the region on trucks before locals ever saw them. She started buying directly from a handful of farmers she knew, opened a tiny shop in Aljezur, and began cooking a short daily menu from whatever came in that morning.

The name came from her co-founder's family land — an apricot orchard near Silves that had fed three generations. It captured exactly what she was after: not a clever brand, but a real place that gives. The weekly box followed when customers kept asking to take "a bit of everything" home. Today Pomar works with eleven growers across the western Algarve and is quietly becoming the region's reference point for eating seasonally.

## What it does

- **Market & kitchen** — A flagship shop and café in Aljezur selling fresh produce, pantry staples, bread and pastries, plus a daily-changing menu of simple seasonal plates (ricotta toast with roasted tomatoes, day-boat fish, whatever the week brings).
- **The Harvest Box** — A weekly subscription box of just-picked produce and a few prepared/pantry items, sized *Solo*, *Casa*, or *Mesa Cheia* (full table) by household. Pause anytime, skip anytime.
- **Grower stories** — Ongoing editorial profiling the farmers and the seasons. The land is the brand.

## Ideology

Pomar believes in **proximity** — that food tastes better and means more when you know the few kilometres it travelled and the hands that grew it. An orchard doesn't rush, and neither does the brand. Three principles guide everything:

1. **Season dictates.** No out-of-season produce, ever. The menu and box change with the harvest, not with demand.
2. **The grower gets named.** Suppliers are partners and they're credited — on shelves, on the box, on the site.
3. **Premium, not precious.** High quality and genuine care, but warm, unfussy, and for everyone — not a luxury performance.

The deeper promise is the *good life* the coast already offers: slowness, real food, sun, community, the long lunch.
